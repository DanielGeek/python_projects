### Concept:
This dish celebrates the umami richness of miso and the earthiness of cauliflower, while also harnessing the vibrant flavors of citrus and the nuttiness of quinoa. It’s a vegetarian main course that’s not only visually appealing but also packed with nutrients.

### Ingredients:
- **For the Cauliflower Steak:**
  - 1 large head of cauliflower
  - 3 tbsp white miso paste
  - 2 tbsp maple syrup
  - 1 tbsp soy sauce
  - 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  - Olive oil for grilling

- **For the Quinoa Base:**
  - 1 cup quinoa (you can mix colors for visual appeal)
  - 2 cups vegetable broth
  - 1 tsp turmeric (for color and flavor)
  - Salt to taste

- **For the Charred Citrus Salsa:**
  - 1 orange, segmented and lightly charred
  - 1 grapefruit, segmented and lightly charred
  - 1 jalapeño, finely diced (adjust based on heat preference)
  - Fresh cilantro, chopped
  - Juice of 1 lime
  - Salt and pepper to taste

### Preparation Steps:
1. **Prepare the Cauliflower Steak:**
   - Preheat your grill or oven to medium-high heat.
   - Slice the cauliflower into thick “steaks” (about 1-inch thick).
   - In a bowl, mix miso paste, maple syrup, soy sauce, and rice vinegar to create a glaze.
   - Brush the cauliflower steaks with olive oil and then coat them with the miso glaze.
   - Grill the steaks for about 5-7 minutes on each side until tender and slightly charred.

2. **Cook the Quinoa:**
   - Rinse the quinoa under cold water.
   - In a pot, combine quinoa, vegetable broth, turmeric, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes or until the liquid is absorbed. Fluff with a fork.

3. **Make the Charred Citrus Salsa:**
   - On a hot grill or skillet, char the orange and grapefruit segments until they have grill marks.
   - In a bowl, combine the charred citrus, jalapeño, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well.

### Plating:
- On a large plate, create a bed of fluffy quinoa.
- Place the grilled miso cauliflower steak on top.
- Generously spoon the charred citrus salsa over the steak.
- Garnish with microgreens for an extra touch of elegance.

### Sustainability Angle:
- Use locally sourced produce and organic ingredients whenever possible.
- This dish is plant-based, reducing your carbon footprint while still being hearty and satisfying.
